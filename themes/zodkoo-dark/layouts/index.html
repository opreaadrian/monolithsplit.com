Does adding features hurt?

Is your team spending more time putting out fires, than making money?

You're working in loss-prevention mode. Every resource available in your team is ready to jump in and end that outage. Deployments are a pain, so you deploy rarely and only after 10 levels of approval. This is a recipe for failure, in today's fast-paced world and you know it!

Sounds like you? Read on!

Having trouble retaining talent? Hiring sucks?

You're losing your employees and candidates are very reluctant to work for you.

Although you respect your employees, offer them competitive salaries and know each one of them by name, they're still leaving. If you would survey them, you'll probably find that they're not dissatisfied with you but there's just no room for them to grow, anymore

If you ask me, they're leaving because they're tired of putting out fires. Your current methods to operate your business are not competitive anymore. The impression you make is that you're only concerned with making money, at the expense of your employees' wellbeing.

You're losing business to your competition!

It feels like you're leaving money on the table. Opportunities walk past you and you can't do anything about it.

Have you ever wondered how is it that your competition is always ahead of you? I'm sure you have. You probably thought that it's because they have more money to reinvest, better developers, better marketing. They might even have a saurcerer somewhere.

Take a look at companies like Amazon, Netflix or Google. They do have huge budgets and a lot of talent. But they don't use that sheer force to push things forward. They work smarter. Ever heard of Amazon's 2-pizza-teams?

There is a better way!

Stop adding features to that old monolith and start creating microservices.

That's right! Although it sounds like nonsense, stopping the feature-bloat on your current application is the way to make more money. Start splitting that huge chunk of code into money-making services!

Remember that mobile application you wanted to build? Did the work to create separate APIs for your data prove too big of a challenge? With microservices you can think of a mobile app, a web app or a desktop app as a simple representation to your data! You could even create a command line interface for the terminal-lover in your customer.

Technology's fast-paced landscape makes it harder and harder to launch features in a 6 months release cycle. With certain companies deploying multiple times a day, it's hard to keep up if you're too far behind the pack.

Microservices enable you to deploy as many times you like and perform experiments that would otherwise seem impossible due to the big "blast radius" a monolithic architecture involves.
